## Recommendations

- Improve Low-Performing Categories
  - Review pricing, promotions, and demand for underperforming categories.
  - Introduce limited-time discounts or seasonal offers to test customer response.
  - Consider reducing inventory or replacing slow-moving items with higher-demand alternatives.

- Increase Wholesale Sales Contribution.
  - Offer bulk discounts or loyalty incentives for wholesale buyers.
  - Target small retailers and bulk buyers with tailored pricing plans.
  - Wholesale contributes only 14.78%, showing growth potential.
 
- Strengthen High-Performing Categories.
  -Invest more in top-revenue categories (₦95K) through better shelf placement and visibility.
  - Bundle best-selling products within these categories to increase basket size.
  - Use cross-selling strategies to pull demand into weaker categories.

- Stabilize Daily Sales Fluctuations
  - Introduce daily or weekly promotions to reduce sharp sales drops.
  - Align restocking with peak sales days to avoid stock-outs.
  - Use historical daily trends to plan staffing and inventory levels.
 
- Maximize High-Performing Products
  - Maintain adequate stock for top products (23K revenue leaders) to avoid missed sales.
  - Promote top products via in-store displays or digital ads.
  - Use these products as entry points for upselling complementary items.
  
- Boost Online Sales Channel
  - Online sales contribute 33.36%, but can grow further.
  - Improve online visibility through social media promotions.
  - Improve online visibility through social media promotions.

- Plan Around Seasonal Trends
  - Sales peak in January and late-year months.
  - Increase inventory and marketing spend during high-demand periods.
  - Use slower months for clearance sales and customer re-engagement campaigns.  

- Maintain Profit Margin Control
  - A 21% profit margin is strong—maintain this by:
  - Monitoring supplier costs.
  - Avoiding excessive discounting.
  - Focusing promotions on high-margin products
